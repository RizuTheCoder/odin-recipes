Biryani

Description

Hyderabadi biryani (also known as Hyderabadi dum biryani) is a style of biryani originating from Hyderabad, India made with basmati rice and meat (mostly goat meat). Originating in the kitchens of the Nizam of Hyderabad, it combines elements of Hyderabadi and Mughlai cuisines. Hyderabad biryani is a key dish in Hyderabadi cuisine and it is so famous that the dish is considered synonymous with the city of Hyderabad.

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice
  • Chicken
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Red Chili Powder
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Garam Masala
  • Salt
  • Lemon Juice
  • Mint Leaves
  • Coriander Leaves
  • Green Chilies
  • Onions
  • Bay Leaves
  • Green Cardamoms
  • Cloves
  • Cinnamon Stick
  • Saffron
  • Milk
  • Ghee
  • Foil

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate the meat with yogurt, spices, herbs, lemon juice, and ginger-garlic paste. Let it rest for at least 1 hour
  3. Fry onions until golden brown and crisp (birista). Set aside.
  4. Boil the rice with whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  5. Layer the biryani in a heavy pot:
    • First, marinated meat at the bottom
    • Then half the fried onions
    • Then parboiled rice
    • Top with saffron milk, ghee, mint, coriander, and remaining onions
  6. Seal the pot with dough or foil and a tight lid.
  7. Cook on dum (low flame) for about 30â€“45 minutes until meat is tender and rice fully cooked.
  8. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.
  9. Gently mix and serve hot with raita or salad.
